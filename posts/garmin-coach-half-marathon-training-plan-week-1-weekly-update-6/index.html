Garmin Coach Half-Marathon Week 1/16 | Weekly Update #6

Jul 19, 2020 in GARMIN COACH HALF-MARATHON TRAINING PLAN • HALF-MARATHON • WEEKLY UPDATE
half-marathon training plan shoes garmin coach garmin connect coach amy hoka one one gaviota 2
4 min read

First week of the Garmin Coach Half-Marathon Training Plan with Coach Amy is done!
And I tried some new shoes.

Because I started on this plan on Tuesday this week had only 3 workouts in it and they were:

  • Tuesday (14th): Benchmark Run
  • Thursday (16th): Run
  • Saturday (18th): Run

Details for my Benchmark Run can be found on my last post linked above (or on the bottom of this posts).
After the Benchmark Run was done, the runs for the rest of the week were generated and a short video about the importance of easy runs and rest days. Which didn’t offer anything new to me, but I assume that is the standard video that also appears on all other of Coach Amys plans.

Thursday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 4.83km | 8:31 to 8:50 min/km
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

The plan seems to have settled on 8:31 to 8:50 min/km pace as my “easy run pace”. Which is probably super slow for everyone else, but feels good to me. I finished the run segment with an average pace of 8:34 min/km. After every run you have to give your run an difficulty rating from the following choices:

  • Very Easy
  • Easy
  • Moderate
  • Hard
  • Very Hard (at least I think that’s what it was)

Since this run was nice and easy, that’s what I rated it as, easy.

Saturday Long Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 8.05km | 8:31 to 8:50 min/km
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

This was probably my longest run so far. I actually made use of garmins route/navigation feature for this run. I planned a route in the Garmin Connect Route Planner, synced it to my watch and selected it during my run. A short distance before every direction change, the watch vibrated and displayed a little arrow to show which direction to continue on. Liked this feature a lot, this way I can stray away from my usual route, without having to keep the whole course in my mind. This is also the first run that I took something to drink with me. For that I used the CAMELBAK Quick Grip Chill Handheld Water Bottle, which is an insulated plastic bottle with a volume of 620ml / 21oz, which has a detachable handgrip with a small bag that fits a phone, id and keys for example. I filled it with water and to be sure an electrolyte tablet, I am a heavy sweater after all. The run itself was ok but i struggled at the end, the legs got heavy.
I had an average pace of 8:35 min/km, so well within the range I should be in. Because I struggled for the last 1.5km I rated it as a moderate difficulty.

These 3 runs put me at a total distance of 17.98km for this week.

Hoke One One Gaviota 2

A friend of mine has these shoes in a similar size to mine. They are stability shoes, which was something new to me in a runnign shoe. He had them also in wide and they supposedly are the stability equivalent to the Hoka’s Bondi line, which I can’t find in wide, but was told to try. The whole stability thing didn’t get in the way at all, they felt great on a short run I did, and you can feel the difference in cushioning to my shoes. Long Story Short, I ordered a pair for myself. Hoke One One even offers to take them back after you have taken them to the streets in a 30 day period, which is awesome.

Next week is going to be for the full 4 runs that I set the training plan to for now. Till then!
