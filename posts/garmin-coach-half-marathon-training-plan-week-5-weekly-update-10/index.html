Garmin Coach Half-Marathon Week 5/16 | Weekly Update #10

Aug 15, 2020 in GARMIN COACH HALF-MARATHON TRAINING PLAN • HALF-MARATHON • WEEKLY UPDATE
half-marathon training plan garmin coach garmin connect coach amy
4 min read

Garmin Coach finally decided to move this training plan into the next stage, but other than new titles for runs, not much changed.

This weeks runs:

  • Monday (10th of August): Tired Run
  • Tuesday (11th of August): Easy Run
  • Thursday (13th of August): Easy Run
  • Saturday (15th of August): Run

Compared to the last 4 weeks, not all workouts were titled as “Run” this week, only Saturdays long run stuck with that. The “Tired Run” is supposed to be a recovery run, being the run after the long run. It gives you the following description in the workout notes: “Give your best effort on tired legs. Don’t overdo it.”
Which in my eyes could also be interpreted as “go all out if you can”, but what i could understand is that you still should go “easy” and it is there to simulate later stages of a race, just that I found that out after the run.
The “Easy Run” is just the same es the normal “Run” the weeks before, same pace window and all.

Monday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 4.83km | Give your best effort on tired legs. Don’t overdo it.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

Since I didn’t yet understand the any of the pacing, I just ran without looking at my watch and ran the “Run” part in a 7:58min/km pace. Overall I ran 5.24km on Monday.

Tuesday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 4.83km | 8:31 to 8:50 min/km | Easy pace.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

The second run of the week moved to Tuesday from Wednesday. Otherwise it was the same old. Distance: 5.22km.

Thursday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 6.44km | 8:31 to 8:50 min/km | Easy pace.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

So the midweek runs also start stepping up in distance!
Distance: 6.99km

Saturday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 12.87km | 8:31 to 8:50 min/km | Easy pace.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

After being at 9.65km long runs for 3 weeks in a row, I had a 3.21km / 2 Miles step up this week. I struggled this run. I still got in a slightly faster average pace than the give pace window with 8:29min/km, but I started to fast and had to take a slow walk towards the end. the run took almost 2 hours, I started early, but once the sun was up and I was over 10km run I was struggling. Still happy I got it done, but I need to pay more attention to my pace in the future. I rated this run as “Hard” because I had to walk for a short while. With these runs going near the 2 hour mark, I feel like I should start thinking about getting some carbs in. So far I have only taken a bottle filled with water and an electrolyte tablet.

Distance: 13.37km

Weekly distance overall was 30.82km/19.15miles, which is more than a 5km step up from last week. This weeks “Tired Run” was on Monday, which I assumed happened because I blocked Sunday off as a no run day. But I actually took that setting out to see what happens for next weeks plan and the Tired Run is still in Mondays. Weird. Is that supposed to be this way?

Feel free to come back next week!
