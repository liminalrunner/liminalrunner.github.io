Garmin Coach Half-Marathon Week 13/16 | Weekly Update #18

Oct 11, 2020 in GARMIN COACH HALF-MARATHON TRAINING PLAN • HALF-MARATHON • WEEKLY UPDATE
half-marathon training plan garmin coach garmin connect coach amy
5 min read

Just 4 weeks to go on this Garmin Coach Training Plan! This week had a Time Trial, with new personal bests and the first long run over the 21.1km half marathon distance.

This weeks runs:

  • Tuesday (6th of October): Tired Run
  • Wednesday (7th of October): Time Trial
  • Thursday (8th of October): Easy Run
  • Saturday (11th of October): Long Run

Monday had an all day rainstorm and while I don’t mind running in a bit of rain, I actually like it, running in a storm is a different beast. That’s the reason why I pushed the run to Tuesday.

Tuesday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 10.46km | Give your best effort on tired legs. Don’t overdo it.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

Distance: 10.77km

Wednesday Run

  • Warm up: 0.80 km | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 1.61km | Time Trial | Give your best effort!
  • Speed Drills 4 repeats:
    • Run: 0.40km | 7:35 to 7:54 min/km | Run hard!
    • Recover: 0.40km | Jog or walk if you need to.
  • Cool down: 1.61km | Walk or jog lightly

Time Trial and Speed Drills all in one run! As I mentioned before on intervals, I am horrible at judging my pace. I ran the first 0.40km at a 4:45 to 4:55 pace, which was way to fast. After I noticed that, I slowed down for 5 seconds and then continued at a 5:40ish pace, slowed further down to a 6:20ish pace when I noticed I still could go faster for the rest and went in a 5:35 pace on the last 0.20km. For those 1.61km aka 1 mile, I had an average pace of 5:38min/km, which translates to 9:04.2 min/mile. This is a new personal best for me, the previous being from the first time trial of this training plan on the 26th of August, which was 6:02min/km or 9:42.8min/mile. Included in this was also a new 1km personal best with 5:27.5min/km with the previous being 5:54.9min/km, also from the same time trial in August. Which was a good improvement on paper, but being a bad judge at pace, I have no idea if these two runs were at an comperable effort. After the time trial I took a minute or two of walking to normalize my breathing and got into the speed repeats, which for the first time was at a tempo near my target “race pace” of 7:35 min/km. Distance: 7.49km

Thursday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 6.44km | 8:31 to 8:50 min/km | Easy pace.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

Distance: 6.87km

Saturday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 22.53km | 8:31 to 8:50 min/km | Easy pace.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

The first run which not only reached the 21.1km for the half-marathon, but went even further than that. I didn’t look at my watch at all and tried to figure out an easy pace. I stopped 4 times for traffic lights (for which i paused the run timer), but other than that I ran the distance without stopping. At the end I was really done. Over the whole 22.53km segment, I averaged a 8:11min/km pace. If the heart rate sensor is somewhat accurate, for the last 1/5th of the segment I was constantly over 150 up to 160 bpm towards the end. This run also was, unsurprisingly, my “personal best” at a half-marathon distance, with it being the only run at this distance so far, with a time of 2:52:42 for the 21.1km distance. Seeing that time and knowing how I felt at the end of it, I am still very worried about achieving that average pace of 7:35 min/km, which the garmin coach tool is very confident in me making that. During the last 5km of the run, I felt my back hurting and noticed that I was running a bit hunched over, should look to stick to a more upright form.
Today is Sunday and I feel that run in my legs and knees. Not hurting, but a bit of soreness, I guess.
I actually ran a half-marathon and in a time that would land me under the 3hour cut-off time which seems to be common at races. I have to say that I ran it on a very flat course, but still, I did the distance. Even if I don’t achieve the goal pace that is set for the training plan, which was the slowest “time goal” I could set as I explained at the start of this training plan, my personal goal was just to be able to actually run a half-marathon at the end of this training plan, finishing in the set time of 2hours40min is more of a bonus for me now.

For what to do after this training plan, I am considering getting some help of a coach, probably online, because I looked near me and couldn’t really find one, to help me set goals and map out a long-term training plan for the future.

Total distance this week: 48.07km
