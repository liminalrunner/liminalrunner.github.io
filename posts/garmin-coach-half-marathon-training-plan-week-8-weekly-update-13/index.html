Garmin Coach Half-Marathon Week 8/16 | Weekly Update #13

Sep 06, 2020 in GARMIN COACH HALF-MARATHON TRAINING PLAN • HALF-MARATHON • WEEKLY UPDATE
half-marathon training plan garmin coach garmin connect coach amy
4 min read

Speed workouts have finally arrived, after 7 weeks!

This weeks runs:

  • Monday (31st of August): Tired Run
  • Tuesday (1st of September): Speed Repeats
  • Thursday (3rd of September): Easy Run
  • Saturday (5th of September): Run

So, speed workouts are here. Weirdly the plan showed me a video about hill repeats instead of speed repeats.

Monday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 8.05km | Give your best effort on tired legs. Don’t overdo it.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

I gonna stop wondering why it is on Monday. This was the longest “non long run” so far. Distance: 8.35km

Tuesday Run

  • Warm up: 1.61km | Walk or jog lightly
  • 7 repeats:
  • Run: 1 min | 6:58 to 7:16 min/km | Run hard!
  • Recover: 1min | Jog or walk if you need to.
  • Cool down: 1.61km | Walk or jog lightly

I had actually trouble getting the pace right in that 1 minute. The pace update on the watch felt too slow and for most intervals I got the pace right just before the interval ended. Otherwise felt good doing something other than easy runs.
Distance: 5.08km

Thursday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 4.83km | 8:31 to 8:50 min/km | Easy pace.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

Same old.
Distance: 5.58km

Saturday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 12.87km | 8:31 to 8:50 min/km | Easy pace.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

This weeks long run was again a step back to last weeks. I now just assume that this is wanted. I got through it surprisingly good. Distance: 13.71km

This weeks total distance was 32.71km. So the weekly distance stayed the same as last week, that might explain because there was a step back in the long run.
Monday was also the last run of August. All runs of August add up to a total distance of 133.13km, which just as a number seems insane to me. I am proud of me for actually going through with this. This is also the half-way point of this training plan. While I just said that I am proud of my progress, I still feel like I am nowhere on track of actually making my goal of running a half-marathon in 2 hours 40 minutes, which is a 7:35min/km pace. The Garmin Coach algorithm is, contrary to me very positive about me progress. They have a gauge for how confident the algorithm is for you being on your way to make your goal time, for me it displays:
Extremely Confident: You’re exceeding plan expectations and may be capable of taking on a more challenging goal.
I haven’t run a single second at that target pace, but I guess we’ll see at the end.

First half done, onto the next one, see you next week.
