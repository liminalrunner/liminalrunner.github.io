Garmin Coach Half-Marathon Week 11/16 | Weekly Update #16

Sep 27, 2020 in GARMIN COACH HALF-MARATHON TRAINING PLAN • HALF-MARATHON • WEEKLY UPDATE
half-marathon training plan garmin coach garmin connect coach amy
4 min read

Last week were only 3 runs, this week were 6!

This weeks runs:

  • Monday (21st of September): Tired Run
  • Tuesday (22nd of September): Speed Repeats
  • Wednesday (23rd of September): Easy Run
  • Friday (25th of September): Easy Run
  • Saturday (26th of September): Long Run
  • Sunday (27th of September): Tired Run

I had a bit more time on my hands this week and the next, for that reason i switched the plan to have 5 instead of 4 runs a week. Because I didn’t know if I had time on Monday, the normal day for the “Tired Run” in the coming week, I did that today on Sunday, just to be sure to get it in.

Monday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 6.44km | Give your best effort on tired legs. Don’t overdo it.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

The distance for the Tired Runs went down compared to last week, but I assume that’s because this week has a run more scheduled. Distance: 6.84km

Tuesday Run

  • Warm up: 1.61km | Walk or jog lightly
  • 6 repeats:
    • Run: 1:30 min | 6:58 to 7:16 min/km | Run hard!
    • Recover: 1:30 min | Jog or walk if you need to.
  • Cool down: 1.61km | Walk or jog lightly

Intervals also got shorter again :(. Distance: 5.32km

Wednesday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 6.44km | 8:31 to 8:50 min/km | Easy pace.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

Distance: 6.83km

Friday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 6.44km | 8:31 to 8:50 min/km | Easy pace.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

Distance: 6.84km

Saturday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 16.09km | 8:31 to 8:50 min/km | Easy pace.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

We are back up to 16.09km for the long run this week. Distance: 16.46km

Sunday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 8.05km | Give your best effort on tired legs. Don’t overdo it.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

Having the Tired Run actually on the day after the Long Run made me feel “tired” in my legs for the first time on one of these runs. The run from the previous day was still noticeable in my legs. But nothing to worry about, after I was about 1km into it, I felt good.
Distance: 8.46km

This was a total distance of 50.74km. With this including originally 5 runs plus 1 from the coming week, I guess that this will be the top end for weekly mileage for this plan. But with this “easy” pace, I felt no problem doing the runs. But I am still very worried about actually getting making the goal time of this training plan with 2:40.

I am enjoying running a lot, the runs are some “me-time”, whether I am just thinking or listening to an audiobook (seriously a problem, might need to join audible or something). I like reading, but the runs also give me some time to get to some books that I didn’t have time to read so far.

I also started thinking about what I want to do once I am done with this training plan. I don’t run to win a race or something, more to get fit/healthy, but I really enjoy the long runs. I could see me setting a very long-time goal, far in the distance, as in 2 years or more out, to try to complete an Ultra Marathon. Just to see what is possible for me to achieve. But for this, I feel like jumping from one 12-16 week training plan to the next, is not the right way to do it. But this is still just a thought, I will thinking about what to do from November on, when this plan is done, in the coming weeks.
