Garmin Coach Half-Marathon Week 15/16 | Weekly Update #20

Oct 25, 2020 in GARMIN COACH HALF-MARATHON TRAINING PLAN • HALF-MARATHON • WEEKLY UPDATE
half-marathon training plan garmin coach garmin connect coach amy
3 min read

Second to last week is done! The taper is here.

This weeks runs:

  • Monday (19th of October): Tired Run
  • Wednesday (21st of October): Speed Repeats
  • Thursday (22nd of October): Easy Run
  • Saturday (24th of October): Long Run
  • Sunday (25th of October): Tired Run

For this week I rescheduled the Tired Run that would usually be tomorrow on monday to today.

Monday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 6.44km | Give your best effort on tired legs. Don’t overdo it.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

Distance: 6.90km

Wednesday Run

  • Warm up: 1.61km | Walk or jog lightly
  • 7 repeats:
    • Run: 1:00 min | 6:58 to 7:16 min/km | Run hard!
    • Recover: 1:00 min | Jog or walk if you need to.
  • 6 repeats:
    • Run: 1:30 min | 6:58 to 7:16 min/km | Run hard!
    • Recover: 1:30 min | Jog or walk if you need to.
  • Cool down: 1.61km | Walk or jog lightly

And back to the not well liked short intervals! Distance: 7.28km

Thursday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 6.44km | 8:31 to 8:50 min/km | Easy pace.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

Distance: 6.98km

Saturday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 9.65km | 8:31 to 8:50 min/km | Easy pace.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

Distance: 10.08km

Sunday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 4.83km | Give your best effort on tired legs. Don’t overdo it.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

Distance: 5.65km

Today was such nice weather, that I decided to do tomorrows run today. Just to try it out before the “Goal Race” next week, I wanted to try to run at a slightly faster pace than the goal pace of 7:35min/km. I ended up averaging 7:06min/km which felt totally fine, I wasn’t totally out of breath or anything like that. Funnily enough, this run also improved my 5k personal best from the end of my 5k training plan in July, by 8 seconds to 35:22, without actually going hard at it. I assume I could probably knock down somewhere between 1 or 2 minutes of that time if I really tried for that distance.
This weeks distance, inlucding todays run, which normally should be counted towards next week, was 36.90km.
This leaves just 2 runs for the final week, a short speed repeats run and the final half-marathon run.
