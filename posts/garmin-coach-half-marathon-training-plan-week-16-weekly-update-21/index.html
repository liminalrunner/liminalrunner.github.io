Garmin Coach Half-Marathon Week 16/16 | Weekly Update #21

Nov 01, 2020 in GARMIN COACH HALF-MARATHON TRAINING PLAN • HALF-MARATHON • WEEKLY UPDATE
half-marathon training plan garmin coach garmin connect coach amy
6 min read

Done with my first half-marathon plan!

This weeks runs:

  • Wednesday (28th of October): Speed Repeats
  • Friday (30th of October): Final Long Run

Only 2 runs counting towards this week, because I already did the Tired Run that was usually on Monday, on Sunday last week.
The last run was first planned for Saturday, as almost every Long Run i did so far, but on Friday, after 2 days of rain the weather opened up and I could free up some time.

Wednesday Run

  • Warm up: 1.61km | Walk or jog lightly
  • 10 repeats:
    • Run: 0:30 min | 6:58 to 7:16 min/km | Run hard!
    • Recover: 0:30 min | Jog or walk if you need to.
  • Cool down: 1.61km | Walk or jog lightly

For this final speed repeat workout, the intervals got even shorter. Hitting a certain pace window in this short interval is just something that feels really hard to do for me. Distance: 4.61km

Friday Run

  • Run: 21.10 km

The final run, that I have been looking forward to, but also was anxious to see if i really could hit the target pace of 7:35 min/km to hit that 2h40min goal time. I achieved that, with an asterisk.
The path I was running is a mix of gravel and concrete and I was going for an out and back run. The path has multiple smaller streams crossing it, which are easily navigated through by using bigger stones in it or a small wooden path over it, for which I would’ve just slowed down, if they would’ve been wet. What I did not anticipate was that there was so much water, that the streams were flooding the way and covering the stones completely. I had to backtrack and find a way were I can jump/cross it safely. For that I stopped my the timer on my watch.
It being autumn and having rained for the last 2 days, the path was covered in a ton of wet leaves. Which wasn’t a problem till around 17km in i slipped and landed on my ass. Had to walk for under a minute after that but got running again to finish it up. There were also some muddy areas from someone driving around with a tractor or some other farming equipment, which I had to slow down a bit for. Overall, my running time was 2h38min50sec, all included other than me looking how to cross the streams. That averages to 7:32min/km. I was just below my target pace/goal time, which I am very happy with.

What I noticed was that stamina wasn’t the problem, but in my knees I was feeling the load starting to stack up about 2/3rds of the way in and it kept building up. They were done after the run and 2 days after that. My lower back also started hurting a bit around the same time, because my form got sloppy and I sturted to hunch my back, which I do way to often during my day behind my desk.

I actually reached to new personal bests with this run, obviously the half-marathon, but also the 10K with a 1h13min12sec.

Weekly distance was 25.71km.

I am planning on writing a final review of my experience with this Garmin Coach Half-Marathon Training plan in the coming weeks.

About 3 weeks ago, I wrote that I was thinking about getting a “real” coach. I was looking for someone who is both an experienced runner and maybe has some sort of scientific background. I don’t care to much to “push my 5k/10k time” to a certain finish time and compare myself to others, because that train has sailed a long time ago, I am starting at age 34 and also, I enjoyed pushing the distance and challenging myself that way. So the coach should also be someone more distance oriented. And lastly also someone affordable. At this point I cannot justify spending 200$ or more a month for coaching. I just want to make clear, that I am not saying that it could not be worth that much.
For the long term, and I mean more like (a) year(s) and not weeks or months, I’ve set my sight on working towards an ultra marathon. Which is a scary thing to do from my perspective, because I am both overweight and my general fitness isn’t good, but I enjoy the challenge of distance far more than I enjoy the challenge of speed, if that makes sense. And for that I want a coach, because I want to get there as injury free and healthy as possible and just jumping from one training plan (that is usually aimed at the “average person”) to the next might work for most people, but for me I want someone I can talk to to make adjustments and help me reach that far fetched goal in a healthy way.
I looked around the internet and got in touch and had a call with one during this week. I was very happy with our conversation and I am looking forward to working with that Coach. For the coming weeks, we agreed to just get me adjusted to the coaches training style, without working towards a goal other than improving my general fitness.

Posts here may be a bit less frequent till the end of the year, but I will keep posting about my progress and as I meantioned above, there will be a review of the Garmin Coach plan coming.
