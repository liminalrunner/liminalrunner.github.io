Weekly Update #4

This was the last week of the 8 week 5k Plan I have been following. The good news, I got my 5k paced run done. The bad news, I am sick/hurt at the moment.

First, here is the weekly overview:

Monday was a tempo run, going through multiple paces, nothing new but good fun.

I did my “timed” 5k run on Thursday because I was not sure if I could get a run in on Friday or Saturday.
The Nike Run Club 5k Training plan gives a whole table of paces for people in different states of fitness/ability. Because it has been a while since I ran i started this plan on the slowest level of their pace table. That translates to

1k pace 5k pace 10k pace recovery pace
7:30 7:55 8:10 9:00

This would translate to a 12:00 mile pace, you can do the rest of the math yourselves. :)
Following this pace, this would set me up with a 39:30 5k time. I am aware that this is super slow. But I am just competing against myself and not against others. For me this is about finishing it and having fun doing it. Getting faster is something that will come with time.

For the run itself I did about an 800m warmup followed by a few minutes of dynamic stretches. In the last weeks I always felt sluggish on my long runs without having a proper warmup because i thought that at an easy recovery speed I don’t need one (the training plan also didn’t mention one, but does in interval/tempo/fartlek/hill workouts). With this warmup I was ready to go, no sluggish feeling!
Just for the hell of it I did it without looking at my watch to get a gps pace estimate.
Keep in mind the table above with the paces I was going for, here are the “personal records” I set this run:


The first km was the 6:37min/km time, up till the 3k mark, i continued running below 7:00 min/km. Around the 3k mark I had to slow down to a slow jog for a around 20 seconds to get my breathing under control. I finished running an 7:10 min/km average for the whole distance, which means I was 4min faster than the pace I was aiming for. On one side, I now know that I can run a 5k faster than the target pace I set for myself which sounds good, on the other side, I couldn’t pace myself at all. As I said, after 3k my breathing was all over the place. This is something I am not happy about. I guess if i kept the pace at around 7:15 min/km I could’ve run the whole 5k still struggling, but without slowing down or have my breathing go out of control.

Friday was an easy recovery run.

But that’s also when I started feeling a pain in my ribs. I had a slightly annoying feeling in my ribs since monday morning, when stretching after waking up. Which i thought was just me having slept in a weird position or something similar and I only felt it when I fully extended my arms which put a lot of pressure in my ribcage.
At the end of the friday run, I had pain on the right side my ribcage while taking deep breaths which later throughout the day also happened on any major movement like sitting down/getting up or lifting something heavy enough for the muscles near the area to engange. After a visit to my GP, I ended up with the diagnosis of it being an inflammation, possibly from some viral infection and a prescription for some pain killers.
Which led to me finding out that ibuprofen doesn’t seem to do a good job for me on this, because as someone who usually falls asleep while lying on my side, that was impossible. I missed a lot of sleep the last 2 days and I have been in pain. No running at the moment. I hope that this will be gone in a few days.

But this is my 5k training plan done and now I have little bit of time to look for what to do next. I mentioned it in a previous post already, I am looking for a longer distance training plan, especially for a half marathon, which seems to be still managable timewise. I have looked at a few, nothing set in stone yet, might continue with the Nike HM plan, or maybe I might give the Garmin Coach Half Marathon feature which you have access to if you have a garmin watch a try, at least for a week or two to see what it will look like, since it seems to adapt itself to your performance. But I am meanwhile also looking at other plans, tons of them out there on the net.

That’s it for this week, thanks for reading. Hope to see you again!.
