Weekly Update #3

Another week gone by. Some unexpected things came up and I only got to do 3 runs:

Monday was a hill interval workout at different speeds, a first for me. I was on a road that according to google maps should have around 6-7% inclination through a vineyard. I didn’t mind going uphill at all, but the fact that I had to go back down just to get up again between every interval was slightly annoying. I don’t like walking or running downhill in general.
Wednesday were flat intervals. I am learning that I really do enjoy intervals.
Friday was long run day with a 5k. Had the same problem as last week, that the first 1 or 2 km it just felt sluggish and I couldn’t get into a comfortable pace. Don’t know what the problem is there, they were both run at an easy pace but it just didn’t feel good at the start. Maybe I need to do some warmup or something.

Coming week is the last of this 5k training plan and I am thinking about what to do after I am done with this plan. I know that I am not fast at all, but haven’t run 5k at an not easy pace, so I don’t really know what my time could be. So going for a faster 5k time could be something to work on. But I feel more drawn to increasing the distance. The 5k training plan I am using now is from Nike and offers Audio Guided Runs for every run, which I liked a lot. I’ve seen that they also offer this kind of plan for 10k, half marathon and marathon. I am thinking about aiming for that half marathon distance. Going from 5k to 21.1k is a lot not just the distance but also the time commitment. Long run days will take hours of my time, which is something I’ll have to consider. I’ll also have to think about hydration and/or nutrition. I never carried anything with me so far, because I knew I would be back home within an hour at the longest. I’ll also look into half marathon training plans that are available on the internet to see how they differ from the Nike one.

That is all, till next week.
