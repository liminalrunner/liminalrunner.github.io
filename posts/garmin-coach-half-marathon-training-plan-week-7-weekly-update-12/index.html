Garmin Coach Half-Marathon Week 7/16 | Weekly Update #12

Aug 30, 2020 in GARMIN COACH HALF-MARATHON TRAINING PLAN • HALF-MARATHON • WEEKLY UPDATE
half-marathon training plan garmin coach garmin connect coach amy
3 min read

This weeks special is a Time Trial! Find out here what happened!

This weeks runs:

  • Monday (24th of August): Tired Run
  • Wednesday (26th of August): Time Trial
  • Thursday (27th of August): Easy Run
  • Saturday (29nd of August): Run

At the start of this week, I only saw the “Tired Run” and the “Time Trial”, not yet the other runs. My guess was that the “Time Trial” will maybe adjust the easy pace or introduce a new run type.

Monday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 4.83km | Give your best effort on tired legs. Don’t overdo it.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

Tired Run was again set to be on a Monday, when I wasn’t tired anymore. I don’t get the logic behind what. Distance: 5.22km

Wednesday Run

  • Warm up: 0.8km | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 1.61km | Time Trial | Give your best effort
  • Cool down: 0.8km | Walk or jog lightly

The Time Trial! Instead of the normal 2 min warm up and cool down, they actually used a distance here, which was new. The actual distance for the time trial equals to a mile. I set 2 new “personal records” in this time trial.

  • 1k in 5:55min
  • 1mi in 9:43min

Yay me.
I ran for 3.69km that day.

Thursday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 6.44km | 8:31 to 8:50 min/km | Easy pace.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

Well, the given pace window is still the same, so the time trial didn’t change that. Distance: 6.85km

Saturday Run

  • Warm up: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly
  • Run: 16.09km | 8:31 to 8:50 min/km | Easy pace.
  • Cool down: 2 min | Walk or jog lightly

A new long run distance today. Previous longest run was 2 weeks ago, which was 3.2km/2miles less than this run. I ran a totally new route today, which had an uphill section that was steeper than i thought, had to slow down there. It also rained on Friday, which made a short passage of a field way very slippery. Other than that, I got on fine, but felt like I was done around the 15km mark. Total run time was 2 hours 30 minutes, for this I took an oat based energy bar with me. I never really tried to eat while running before, so this was new. I had no “stomach troubles” with it, just eating it while running was weird. Total distance for this run: 16.84km

This brings this weeks total to 32.6km/20.26miles, which is the longest distance I have covered in a week so far.
